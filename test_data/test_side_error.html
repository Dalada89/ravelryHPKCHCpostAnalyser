    Match 2

    September 23-October 14

    The Quidditch referees are all gathered, waiting for TruthVictor who’s a bit late. Brombeere looks at her watch. “She told me she would be on time!”

    Ref No1ComesFromWest shakes her head. “I don’t know, she was headed in another direction when I saw her ten minutes ago.”

    Just then they hear footsteps and TruthVictor enters, holding a couple of old books in her arms: “Look what I found! This is really interesting,” she said.

    “And hello to you, too,” replies lovetosnorkel. “Where have you been?”

    “In the library! You know how I’ve been investigating the Witches and Wizards in the Viking age? I’ve found some really cool documents that shows they did play some form of Quidditch!”

    “That’s very interesting indeed!” Brombeere says with a bright smile. “Come on, what did you find?”

    “Here!” TruthVictor says. “This is so cool.” She reads excitedly from the book: “’The bat was such that it could broken in anger, and that it could be mended on the spot.’” She looks at them, expecting a reaction, but the others just look blankly at her until suzla suddenly realizes: “Ah! ‘Mended on the spot’, that sounds like magic, right?”

    “Exactly,” TruthVictor says. “This sounds like a magic game indeed! And I found evidence of the bludgers, too. Listen: ‘The ball was hard enough that when thrown in anger at another player, it could cause a bleeding injury. And if thrown with enough force, it could knock over another player.’”

    The others are all listening intently now, as she progresses: “The playing field was usually near a pond. Some modern scholars have suggested that the game was played on the surface of a frozen pond. However, the “ice” is actually svell, a word that implies uneven or lumpy ice and is usually applied to ice that has formed on land, rather than over a body of water.“

    TruthVictor looks around. “I think it’s pretty safe to say this was a very daring game if played on foot. Can we imagine that they actually didn’t? That they were already using brooms? In that case, we’re looking at a very interesting case of early Quidditch.”

    Ref okimom nods: “This is all very interesting, thanks for sharing.”

    “Oh, I almost forgot the last points: the game, it says, could go on for days or be over very fast, and the word knattleikr means ball catcher: my theory is that this means the goal of the game is to catch the, well, the golden snitch!?”

    Everybody gasps: they have discovered another part of Quidditch history and they are all very excited, and proud of TruthVictor!

    For this match: Inspired by the bulky or uneven ice the game was played on, craft something with a texture. This can be ribbing, seed stitches, lace, or cables, or just a textured yarn. For Non-Rav craft options, you can use textured paper or fabric or anything you can think of that ends up having a nice texture! You can earn 26 base points for this match. Yardage and awesomeness bonus points are also in play.

    Non-Rav crafts will happily be accepted. Non-Ravelry crafts will be accepted without prior approval as long as they abide by the Non-Rav craft guidelines and fit the prompt.

    Projects should be started and finished within the match dates. WIPs and Partials will not be accepted and should be taken elsewhere.

    Please follow the General Rules of Conduct & Crafting and What Counts as Finished sections of our Student Handbook. Use the standard header for all submissions.
